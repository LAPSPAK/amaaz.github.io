About MySelf

I have recently obtained my masters in Applied Mathematics and Informatics from Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology.
My advisors are Prof. Alexander I. Galushkin (late), Prof. Eduard D. Avedian, Ilya Voronkov. Andrei Raigorodskii was my program coordinator.

My primary research interest lies in the management of intellectual property and technology-based entrepreneurship. I have a particular interest in solving issues related to co-ownership by universities on technical inventions and the impact of the Massive Open Online Courses(MOOCs) on marginalised community and innovation.

Broadly my previous area of research is natural language processing and computational linguistics. More specifically, my primary research interest lies in open information extraction from text and its applications to text processing. Departing from these areas, I have also worked on the problems of Text classification for customer’s reviews of text. In parallel to my main focus, I have also worked on the semantic analysis. My other interests lie in data mining and artificial intelligence.

I was awarded a Russian Federation Fellowship in 2015 for higher studies.

In my spare time, I love to get in touch with nature because I reckon distances and differences are the secret tonics of creativity. I always try to get ready for the new experience and understand the beauty of nature. I like to develop social attraction with people and know about their valuable information. I am a very open minded person with positive attitude and love to meet open minded people too.
